Executive Summary

This comprehensive analysis examines how markets interact with premarket high and low levels following major economic events. By analyzing 350 events from 2015-2025, we uncover critical patterns in market behavior, including the frequency of touching premarket levels and the probability of returning to opposite levels. These insights provide valuable trading opportunities and risk management strategies for market participants.

Key Market Behavior Patterns

Balanced Level Distribution

Markets show remarkable balance in touching premarket levels, with 51.1% touching the high level and 48.9% touching the low level. This near-perfect distribution suggests that premarket levels act as natural magnets for price action regardless of market direction.

Low Return Probability

Only 37.4% of events result in the market returning to the opposite premarket level after the initial touch. This indicates that premarket levels often act as strong support/resistance zones that hold after being tested.

Event Type Variations

Different economic events show distinct patterns: CPI events have the highest high-level touch rate (56.0%), while PPI events show the highest return-to-opposite probability (41.2%). NFP events fall in the middle with balanced behavior.

Market Condition Impact Analysis

High Return Probability Conditions

4-8% Market Conditions: Show the highest return-to-opposite probability at 61.1%, indicating that moderate inflation environments create more volatile price action around premarket levels.

Low Return Probability Conditions

0-100K NFP & 0-2% PPI: These conditions show the lowest return probabilities (6.7% and 8.3% respectively), suggesting that weak economic data creates stronger support/resistance at premarket levels.

Yearly Trends and Evolution

Increasing High-Level Preference

Recent years (2021-2023) show a trend toward higher high-level touch rates, with 2023 reaching 63.6%. This suggests evolving market behavior patterns that may reflect changing economic conditions or market structure.

Volatility Patterns

2017 and 2020 show the highest return-to-opposite probabilities (55.9% and 44.1% respectively), indicating periods of higher market volatility and less reliable premarket level support/resistance.

Trading Strategy Implications

Level-Based Entry Strategies

With 62.6% of events not returning to opposite levels, premarket levels provide reliable entry points. Consider entering positions when price approaches premarket highs/lows, with stops beyond these levels.

Event-Specific Adjustments

Adjust expectations based on event type: CPI events favor high-level touches (56.0%), while PPI events show higher return probabilities (41.2%). NFP events provide balanced opportunities.

Market Condition Filters

Use market conditions as filters: 4-8% PPI environments offer the highest return probabilities (61.1%), while 0-100K NFP environments provide the strongest level support (6.7% return rate).

Risk Management

Given the 37.4% return-to-opposite probability, position sizing should account for potential reversals. Consider reducing position size in high-return-probability environments.

Behavioral Finance Insights

Anchoring and PreMarket Levels

The consistent 51.1%/48.9% split in high/low touches suggests that premarket levels act as psychological anchors. Traders and algorithms likely use these levels as reference points for decision-making.

Support/Resistance Psychology

The 62.6% hold rate indicates that premarket levels function as genuine support/resistance zones. Once tested, these levels often provide reliable boundaries for subsequent price action.

Volatility and Level Reliability

Higher return probabilities in certain market conditions (4-8% PPI) suggest that increased volatility can reduce the reliability of premarket levels as support/resistance zones.

Conclusion

Premarket levels serve as critical reference points for market behavior following economic events. The analysis reveals a balanced distribution of high/low touches with a strong tendency for levels to hold after being tested. Event type and market conditions significantly influence these patterns, providing traders with valuable insights for strategy development and risk management.
