Understanding Gap Size Categories

Not all gaps are created equal. Our analysis of 1,929+ trading scenarios reveals five distinct gap size categories, each with unique characteristics and optimal trading strategies. Understanding these categories is the foundation of successful gap trading.

Gap Size Categories Overview

  • Micro Gaps (0.15-0.35%): High fill rate, moderate reversal rate
  • Small Gaps (0.35-0.5%): Moderate fill rate, lower reversal rate
  • Standard Gaps (0.5-1%): Lower fill rate, continuation favored
  • Large Gaps (1-1.5%): Low fill rate, strong momentum
  • Momentum Gaps (1.5%+): Very low fill rate, continuation trades

Micro Gaps (0.15-0.35%): The Sweet Spot for Fading

Micro gaps represent the most reliable fading opportunities in gap trading. With fill rates approaching 80%, these small gaps offer consistent reversal setups with manageable risk.

Micro Gap Trading Strategy

1

Identify Micro Gap

Look for gaps between 0.15% and 0.35% at market open. These are the most common and reliable gap sizes.

2

Wait for Fill

Don't trade immediately. Wait for the gap to fill (average 44-47 minutes). This increases reversal probability.

3

Execute Fade Trade

For down gaps: Buy after fill, target 0.22% upward move. For up gaps: Sell after fill, target 0.25% downward move.

4

Risk Management

Use -0.2% stop-loss. Best on Wednesday (56.76% reversal rate for down gaps, 76.47% for up gaps).

Small Gaps (0.35-0.5%): The Transition Zone

Small gaps represent a critical transition point in gap trading. With fill rates dropping to around 59%, these gaps require more careful analysis and often favor continuation trades over fading.

Small Gap Trading Strategy

Continuation Strategy (Recommended)

  • Down Gaps: Short after open, target 0.26% downward move within 30 minutes
  • Up Gaps: Buy after open, target 0.23% upward move within 30 minutes
  • Stop-Loss: 0.3% for both directions
  • Best Days: Monday (41.18% reversal rate for down gaps, 40% for up gaps)

Fading Strategy (Selective)

  • Only fade on Monday due to higher reversal rates
  • Use tighter stop-loss (-0.15%) due to lower reversal probability
  • Target smaller moves (0.20%) for better risk/reward

Standard Gaps (0.5-1%): Continuation Territory

Standard gaps are the most frequent gap size and clearly favor continuation trades. With fill rates around 45-50% and low reversal rates, these gaps require a different approach.

Standard Gap Trading Strategy

Continuation Strategy (Primary)

  • Down Gaps: Short after open, target 0.31% downward move within 30 minutes
  • Up Gaps: Buy after open, target 0.25% upward move within 30 minutes
  • Stop-Loss: 0.4% for down gaps, 0.3% for up gaps
  • Best Days: Tuesday for down gaps (38.10% reversal rate), Friday for up gaps (37.50%)

Fading Strategy (Limited)

  • Only consider fading on specific days with higher reversal rates
  • Use wider stop-loss (-0.3%) due to larger gap size
  • Target smaller moves (0.20%) for better risk management

Large Gaps (1-1.5%) and Momentum Gaps (1.5%+): Momentum Trading

Large and momentum gaps represent the strongest market moves and should almost always be traded in the gap direction. With fill rates below 30% and minimal reversals, these gaps offer high-probability continuation setups.
