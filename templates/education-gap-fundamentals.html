Introduction to Gap Analysis

Gap analysis is one of the most powerful tools in a trader's arsenal. Understanding how gaps work, why they occur, and how to trade them can significantly improve your trading performance. This comprehensive guide will teach you everything you need to know about gap analysis fundamentals.

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Understand what gaps are and how they form
  • Learn to identify different types of gaps
  • Master gap fill rate analysis
  • Develop gap trading strategies
  • Implement proper risk management

Lesson 1: What Are Gaps?

A gap occurs when the opening price of a security is significantly different from the previous day's closing price. This creates a "gap" in the price chart where no trading occurred.

How Gaps Form

  • After-hours news: Earnings reports, economic data, or company announcements
  • Market sentiment shifts: Changes in investor psychology overnight
  • Global events: International news affecting markets
  • Technical factors: Stop-loss orders and algorithmic trading

üìà Real Example

Scenario: A company reports earnings after market close. The stock closed at $100, but opens the next day at $95 due to disappointing results.

Result: A 5% down gap is created between $100 and $95.

Lesson 2: Types of Gaps

1. Common Gaps

These are small gaps that occur frequently and usually fill quickly. They're often caused by normal market noise and don't indicate significant trend changes.

2. Breakaway Gaps

These occur when price breaks out of a consolidation pattern with significant volume. They often signal the start of a new trend and may not fill for extended periods.

3. Runaway Gaps

Also called "continuation gaps," these occur in the middle of a strong trend and indicate the trend is likely to continue.

4. Exhaustion Gaps

These occur near the end of a trend and often signal a reversal is coming. They're characterized by high volume and rapid price movement.

üí° Key Insight

Small gaps (0.5-1.0%) are more likely to fill than large gaps (3%+). This is a crucial pattern for gap trading strategies.

Lesson 3: Gap Fill Rate Analysis

Understanding gap fill rates is essential for successful gap trading. Our data analysis reveals important patterns:

Size Matters

  • 0.5-1.0% gaps: 70-75% fill rate
  • 1.0-1.5% gaps: 60-65% fill rate
  • 1.5-2.0% gaps: 50-55% fill rate
  • 3.0%+ gaps: Less than 40% fill rate

Day of the Week Patterns

  • Tuesday: Highest fill rates across all gap sizes
  • Monday: Good fill rates for down gaps
  • Friday: Excellent fill rates for small down gaps

üìä Data Example

Tuesday up gaps (1.0-1.5%): 78.38% fill rate

Friday down gaps (0.5-1.0%): 75.68% fill rate

Large gaps (3.0%+): Consistently below 40% fill rate

Lesson 4: Gap Trading Strategies

Strategy 1: Mean Reversion (Gap Fade)

This strategy bets that the gap will fill, especially for small gaps with high fill rates.

üìã Mean Reversion Steps

  1. Identify a small gap (0.5-1.5%)
  2. Wait for the first 15-30 minutes of trading
  3. Look for signs the gap won't expand further
  4. Enter a position opposite to the gap direction
  5. Set profit target at gap fill level
  6. Use tight stop-loss for risk management

Strategy 2: Momentum Trading

This strategy trades in the direction of the gap, especially for larger gaps or strong volume.

üìã Momentum Trading Steps

  1. Identify a gap with strong volume
  2. Wait for confirmation of gap direction
  3. Enter in the direction of the gap
  4. Use the gap as a reference for support/resistance
  5. Set profit targets beyond the gap

Lesson 6: Technical Analysis Integration

Key Indicators for Gap Trading

  • Volume: High volume confirms gap direction
  • Support/Resistance: Previous levels influence gap behavior
  • Moving Averages: Use as dynamic support/resistance
  • RSI: Oversold/overbought conditions
  • VWAP: Volume-weighted average price

Chart Patterns to Watch

  • Gap and Go: Price gaps and continues in same direction
  • Gap and Fill: Price gaps then returns to fill the gap
  • Gap and Extend: Price gaps, fills, then continues in original direction

Practical Application

Now let's apply what we've learned to a real trading scenario:

üéØ Complete Trading Example

Setup: Tuesday morning, stock gaps down 0.8% on earnings news

Analysis: Small gap, Tuesday pattern, high fill probability

Strategy: Mean reversion fade

Entry: After 20 minutes, price shows signs of stabilization

Position: Long position at $95.50

Target: Gap fill at $96.30 (previous close)

Stop: $95.00 (0.5% risk)

Result: Gap fills by 11 AM, profit target hit

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Avoid These Common Errors

  • Chasing large gaps: They have low fill rates
  • No stop-loss: Always protect your capital
  • Ignoring volume: Volume confirms gap validity
  • Over-trading: Not every gap is tradeable
  • Ignoring market conditions: Context matters
