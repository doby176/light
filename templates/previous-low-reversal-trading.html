What is Previous Low Reversal Trading?

Previous Low Reversal Trading is a sophisticated strategy that capitalizes on the market's tendency to bounce off established support levels. When a stock price touches a previous low (a significant support level), it often creates a reversal opportunity as buyers step in to defend that level.

However, not all previous low touches are created equal. Our data analysis reveals that timing and position analysis are crucial for maximizing success rates and minimizing risk.

🎯 The Perfect Setup

  • Touch Type: Previous Low
  • Optimal Time: 14:00 (2 PM)
  • Best Days: Tuesday & Wednesday
  • Position: Between Previous High and Low
  • Target: 0.6-0.9% profit
  • Stop Loss: -0.5%

The Critical Role of Position Analysis

Perhaps the most important factor in this strategy is understanding the open position when the price touches a Previous Low. This single factor can make the difference between a 95% success rate and an 8% success rate.

✅ Between Previous High and Low

Success Rate: 93.70% Average Reversal: 0.3554% Risk Level: Low

When the price touches a Previous Low but remains between the previous high and low levels, it indicates strong support is holding. This creates the ideal setup for a reversal trade.

❌ Below Previous Low

Success Rate: 8.18% Average Loss: -0.4634% Risk Level: High

When the price breaks below a Previous Low, it indicates support has failed. This creates a high-risk setup that should be avoided for reversal trades.
